About

  • What is Cardiothoracic Ratio (CTR)

    CTR stands for Cardiothoracic Ratio, which is a measurement used to assess the size of the heart relative to the size of the chest. The cardiothoracic ratio is measured on a PA chest x-ray, and is the ratio of maximal horizontal cardiac diameter to maximal horizontal thoracic diameter (inner edge of ribs/edge of pleura).

  • What does CTR tell us ?

    The CTR can still be used as an important parameter that can be easily determined in establishing enlargement of the heart. A measurement >0.50 indicates that the heart is enlarged, which can be a sign of various underlying medical conditions such as heart failure, hypertension, or valvular heart disease. Therefore, CTR can provide important information about the patient's cardiac health and help guide their treatment plan.

  • The usage of CTR for kidney dialysis patients

    Patients with chronic heart failure, long-term hypertension, or chronic kidney disease undergoing hemodialysis may often have a CTR greater than 0.5, indicating enlargement of the heart. This not only increases the risk of respiratory distress or hypotension during dialysis, but may also lead to arrhythmia. It also suggests that the patient may have excessive fluid in their body, requiring stricter fluid restriction or an increase in the amount of fluid removed during dialysis.

  • Aortic calcification

    Aortic calcification refers to the buildup of calcium in the walls of the aorta, which is the body's largest artery that carries oxygen-rich blood from the heart to the rest of the body. Symptoms of aortic calcification are usually not noticeable until the condition is advanced and can lead to complications such as aneurysms, stroke, heart attack, and peripheral artery disease. Diagnosis of aortic calcification is usually done through imaging tests such as a chest X-ray, CT scan, or MRI.
